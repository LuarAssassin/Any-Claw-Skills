### General daily targets (adults, approximate)

- Calories: 1,800-2,400 kcal (varies by age, sex, activity level)
- Protein: 0.8-1.2 g per kg of body weight
- Fiber: 25-30 g
- Water: 2.0-3.0 liters (including water from food)

## Medication Scheduling Best Practices

### Timing conventions

- **Once daily**: Choose a consistent time. Morning is common for most
  medications unless the label specifies otherwise.
- **Twice daily**: Space doses approximately 12 hours apart (e.g., 08:00
  and 20:00).
- **Three times daily**: Space approximately 8 hours apart (e.g., 07:00,
  15:00, 23:00).
- **With food**: Take during or immediately after a meal to reduce
  stomach irritation.
- **On an empty stomach**: Take at least 1 hour before or 2 hours after
  eating for proper absorption.

### Reminder strategies

1. Link doses to daily routines (breakfast, bedtime) for consistency.
2. Set reminders 5-10 minutes before the target time.
3. Use a follow-up reminder 30 minutes later to confirm the dose was taken.
4. For medications that must not be taken together, stagger reminders by
   at least 1 hour.

### Missed dose guidance (general)

- If a dose is missed and it is close to the next scheduled dose, skip
  the missed dose. Do not double up.
- Always consult the prescribing physician or pharmacist for
  medication-specific missed-dose instructions.

## Health Metric Normal Ranges (Adults)

| Metric | Normal Range | Unit | Notes |
|--------|-------------|------|-------|
| Resting heart rate | 60-100 | bpm | Athletes may be lower (40-60) |
| Blood pressure (systolic) | 90-120 | mmHg | Measured at rest |
| Blood pressure (diastolic) | 60-80 | mmHg | Measured at rest |
| Body temperature | 36.1-37.2 | C (97.0-99.0 F) | Oral measurement |
| Blood oxygen (SpO2) | 95-100 | % | Below 90% is a medical concern |
| Fasting blood glucose | 70-100 | mg/dL | Pre-diabetic: 100-125 |
| BMI | 18.5-24.9 | kg/m^2 | BMI is a rough screening tool |
| Respiratory rate | 12-20 | breaths/min | At rest |
| Sleep duration | 7-9 | hours/night | For adults aged 18-64 |
| Daily steps | 7,000-10,000 | steps | General fitness guideline |

### Tracking tips

- Measure blood pressure at the same time each day, ideally morning
  and evening, after sitting quietly for 5 minutes.
- Record resting heart rate first thing in the morning before getting
  out of bed for the most consistent readings.
- Track trends over weeks rather than reacting to single readings.

## When to Seek Medical Attention

### Call emergency services immediately if experiencing:

- Chest pain or pressure lasting more than a few minutes
- Sudden difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Signs of stroke: sudden numbness or weakness on one side, confusion,
  trouble speaking, sudden severe headache, vision changes
- Severe allergic reaction: swelling of face/throat, difficulty
  breathing, rapid pulse
- Loss of consciousness or unresponsiveness
- Uncontrolled or heavy bleeding
- Suicidal or self-harm thoughts (also contact a crisis line)

### See a healthcare provider soon if experiencing:

- Fever above 39.4 C (103 F) lasting more than 2 days
- Persistent vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 48 hours
- Unexplained weight loss of more than 5% in a month
- New or worsening pain that does not respond to over-the-counter relief
- Symptoms that progressively worsen over several days
- Any unusual symptom that causes significant concern
